## Why Follow This Blog

The Citizen Lab occupies a unique position at the intersection of technical security research, human rights advocacy, and policy work. Their investigations into commercial spyware have exposed some of the most sophisticated exploitation techniques in the world while simultaneously protecting journalists, activists, and civil society from state-sponsored surveillance.

## Key Topics Covered

### Commercial Spyware Analysis
- **NSO Group/Pegasus**: Deep technical analysis of the world's most notorious spyware
- **Zero-Click Exploits**: Understanding exploits that require no user interaction
- **iOS/Android Exploitation**: Mobile platform compromise techniques
- **Spyware Infrastructure**: Mapping command and control networks
- **Attribution**: Connecting spyware deployments to specific operators

### Digital Surveillance
- **Nation-State Operations**: Government surveillance programs
- **Targeted Attacks**: Attacks against journalists and activists
- **Network Monitoring**: Internet filtering and surveillance infrastructure
- **Phishing Campaigns**: Sophisticated social engineering operations

### Internet Censorship
- **Content Filtering**: National-level internet censorship
- **App Store Restrictions**: Platform-level content control
- **VPN Blocking**: Circumvention tool interference
- **Information Controls**: Broader patterns of digital authoritarianism

### Policy & Accountability
- **Export Controls**: Surveillance technology trade regulations
- **Legal Frameworks**: Human rights implications of spyware
- **Corporate Accountability**: Holding spyware vendors responsible
- **Victim Support**: Assisting targets of surveillance

## Impact Research

- **Digital Surveillance**: Mapping the infrastructure of global surveillance companies.
